# Design

## Direction

This is a collectible desktop experience for systems architecture. It should feel closer to an architectural exhibition than to a conventional website.

The visual language combines infrastructure, control rooms, enterprise systems, and restrained architectural luxury. It should be quiet, confident, timeless, and precise.

## Palette

- Void: `#070807`
- Carbon: `#0d0f0d`
- Charcoal: `#151611`
- Olive black: `#181d17`
- Deep olive: `#273227`
- Mineral: `#485044`
- Sage: `#8f9a85`
- Stone: `#c7c3b4`
- Paper: `#eee9dc`
- Gold: `#c8a96a`
- Amber: `#e4c785`
- Rust: `#8c5f3f`

The palette is dark and premium with warm metallic accents, mineral greens, and concrete neutrals. Avoid neon treatment, bright gradients, and decorative visual effects.

## Typography

Use a high-contrast display serif for monumental statements and Sora for technical clarity. Headings need space to breathe. Body copy should be readable, measured, and direct.

## Imagery

Imagery must show real infrastructure and architectural atmosphere: enterprise data center, cooling and compute hardware, network cabling, automation controls, and cinematic architecture. All images are local and documented in `IMAGE-SOURCES.md`.

## Layout

Avoid a repeated card-grid formula. The page is a sequence of chapters:

- Hero: large systems statement, transparent logo, infrastructure image, and strong CTAs.
- Positioning: intimate narrative and architectural shadow.
- Expertise: interactive index and animated system-line diagram.
- Capabilities: structured capability matrix.
- Approach: phase rail over infrastructure.
- Studies: architectural exhibition with selectable system studies.
- Why Khalid: clear principles.
- Insights: concise operating notes.
- Contact: the strongest final emotional moment.

## Motion

Motion should reveal alignment, light, and structure. Use controlled scroll reveals, image masking, selectable panels, and system-line drawing. Avoid cursor gimmicks, excessive animation, neon effects, and heavy 3D.
